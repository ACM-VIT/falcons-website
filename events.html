ENGINEERS'
DAY'16

Engineers day is celebrated every September at VIT University, Vellore in commemoration of the birthday of Bharat Ratna Visvesvaya (September 15, 1861). On this day students from all branches display their engineering prowess in the form of Baja (off road) race cars, radio controlled aircraft models, new architectural designs and many more. The teams explain their ideas to professors, students, and judges who later select the winning stalls.

It’s an exciting day for all the teams, who utilize this opportunity to showcase their talents to professors and key people from various companies. For students (especially first years) this is a golden opportunity as it allows them to get involved and actually apply their textual knowledge in the real world.

Team Assailing Falcons has received a lot of appreciation over the last few years for the UAV’s, with which the team participates annually in the SAE Aerodesign competition held in the USA. The team won the first prize last year (2015) and received the consolation prize this year (2016).

GRAVITAS
EXPO'16

GraVITas expo 2016 marked the display of our 2016 competition, SAE Aero Design plane Carvao. The expo saw a huge crowd of students turn up for seeing the display along with some other pre-graVITas display models. Students along with renowned faculties fro, different engineering streams witnessed the display. Our complete team was present at the event to clear the doubts of the students and faculties regarding the flying dynamics of the planes. Seeing the remarkable improvements that the team has witnessed in the due course of seven years since its inception, many of the students showed keen interest in joining the team and learning more about team dynamics and aero-modeling. The graVITas expo helped in boosting our moral so as to achieve much bigger feats in aeromodelling at R/C level realizing the enthusiasm that was prevalent among the audience who saw our last aircraft.

NATIONAL
TECH EXPO

Atmos’16, BITS Pilani Hyderabad Campus was a phenomenal environment with innovations and creations buzzing around the campus. The subtleness of the fest and beauty of the campus had attracted budding engineers from all corners of the country. Team Assailing Falcons, was a part of the competition “Tech Expo” in which students had to showcase their projects or startups which would benefit the society and technological front of our country. Hundreds of teams showed up with their myriad and fascinating projects to prove their technical prowess. It was tough competition as the prize funding was an integral part of any project or start-up.

We were nothing but prepared to win this competition. Our team regressively worked day and night to prepare Carvao for the competition. Carvao was now ready to perform various socially benefitting operations like disaster aid, thermal imaging, and relief package dropping. As a bonus, Carvao could be prepared for covert military or navy operations. We humbly showcased our plane to all the students, faculties and the companies who visited us and we made sure they left with an aw-inspiring comment. It was challenging yet entertaining to answer all the questions posed to us, as people from all walks of life were visiting us. The main sponsor of the event, CEO of WAT Consult, was impressed with what we had to showcase.

Logistics and finances were a big issue for the team as Hyderabad was far and Carvao was big. However, all these problems were reduced to a nibble when we were given the Second Best Project Award and twenty thousand rupees as funding. Accolades from our University and faculties flowed in hearing this great news. The team is now motivated for such competitions and ready to face any challenge. We shall pave the way until the challenge becomes an opportunity!

SAE '15'

Right from the start, sae 2015 was a highly competitive event. Hosted in Tampa city of Florida, sae 2015 was a mega event.

The plane we had designed for this competition was unique and eye catching for everyone throughout the event. A twin fin biplane, with double boom integration and an enormous 21 inch propeller was something very radical. Being a completely radical design, which no team had tried before, the nautilus was a prime attraction to all teams, organisers and sponsors.

This didn’t deter us one bit. Falcons has been outperforming themselves ever since its formation 6 years ago. This year too, with a gross payload carried of 30 lbs. We received constant guidance from our seniors throughout the event.

The first 6 days of the teams stay in Florida, the team dedicated to preparing a few spares and testing of the plane and its different systems. Various minor changes and fine tuning was done during this period.

The first day of the competition included the technical presentation and the technical inspection. The team performed resplendently in the technical presentation and scored *** points and was placed and with *** rank after this tier of the competition. Then in the technical inspection, our plane was heavily praised for its unique design and structures. A few minor safety measures were suggested, the plane was then given a certification after those changes were implemented.

On the second day of the competition the flight rounds began and the first flight round was very successful. The takeoff and landing of the plane was heavily appreciated by applause by all the spectators. By the second round of flight, the temperature had risen, causing a thermal sink to be formed at the end of the runway. This caused almost all planes to either crash or have a failed attempt to take off. Our plane took off the ground successfully but could not sustain flight. The plane faced a hard landing but on inspection, it was observer that the sturdy and robust structure of the plane had withstood the hard landing and there was no damage at all.

The third and fourth rounds were conducted on the third day of the event. After performing succefully, in these rounds, team assailing falcons was placed at the 11th position internationally and 1st in the Asia pacific region. The event as a whole was a very enlightening experience. The amount of knowledge and experience that the team developed during the competition through interaction with other teams and officials from the sponsor companies was immense. Learning from their experience during the event through events that happened to them and which they observed in other teams, helped them guide and take forward the next team of juniors.

All in all, sae 2015 was a huge success for team assailing falcons.

SAE '16

The “mela” or as you would call it a fair of rc aircrafts and its young enthusiasts was about to begin as Team ASSAILING FALCONS touched down in the City Of Angels on the eve of SAE Aerodesign 2016. As hyped as the event is, it didn’t fail to live upto its expectations even before it began.

It kicked off with a traditional presentations’ session wherein you would put forward your efforts in front of an inquisitive panel of judges. Falcons were all suited up ready to get into the game and come out with flying colours. We proved our mettle when they appreciated the amount of efforts we had put in for the first ever advanced aircraft built by our team. The day ended on a high when both Carvao and Cirio passed the Technical Inspections and we were officially invited for Flyoffs

Sleepless for the entire night, a feeling of nervousness was fast approaching as was the competition

A beautiful Saturday for flyoff at 8am, we arrived at our launch station early in the morning to get the necessary briefings from our pilot, accordingly we proceeded to make that count and get a result. Slowly and steadily all the workstations were occupied and we were ready to go into the first day of flight. The competition took off with the micro class aircrafts gaining momentum and steadily moved onto regular and advanced rounds. We were able to see the fierce competition we had in front of us in the form Warsaw Institute Of Technology who had retained their title for 3 consecutive years

The moment we were all waiting for had finally arrived, Carvao was waiting at the flight line all geared up to take to the American sky. Triggering itself to its max potential, on it went to sail in the sky as the Air Boss blew up the whistle. Lifting its wings like a beautiful bird it took off and with it took off our hardwork and dreams. Climbing like a giant bird it raised to a height of about 400ft within a matter of seconds which was a record second to none in the entirety of SAE’s long history. Definitely a proud moment for us when it achieved its mission statement of humanitarian aid by dropping an aid package from the sky by a completely autonomous system designed to meet its requirement.

The competition went on for four rounds with a close competition up top. In the end we secured a 7th rank internationally and 1st in Asia. Warsaw came out on top yet again with their Baccarat and secured their top position again. It was an eventful day ending with the prize distribution ceremony.

A lot of dreams took off and a whole lot of others were inspired to achieve even greater heights for the next year. Undoubtedly it was again a successful year for the SAE Aerodesign committee who had organised the entire competition. The event ended after 3 days and we were finally on our way back home leaving the City of Angels with loads of memories to look upto.
